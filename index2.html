Jordan 1

Banned by the NBA in 1985, Michael Jordan was charged $5,000 per game for wearing the Air Jordan 1 ‘Black/Red' because they didn’t include the colour white which was part of the Chicago Bulls’ official team colours. Used as a marketing tactic, Nike crafted the ‘Banned’ campaign around the sneakers to launch and promote the Air Jordan line. The sneaker was retroed in 1994, 2011, 2013 and 2016. The 2011 pair features an ‘X’ on the heel paying homage to the 'Banned' nickname.

R10,000

Jordan 2

2022 was unofficially dubbed the Year of the Jordan 2, as Jordan Brand brought back the classic Air Jordan 2 Chicago in its true OG form. The Air Jordan 2 Chicago reps the same historic colorway worn by Michael Jordan during his 1986-1987 season with the Bulls. The sneaker features white reptile-embossed leather uppers, Varsity Red reptile spine design on the heel, and Varsity Red signature Wings logo on the tongue. A black midsole and "NIKE" debossed branding on the heel tab ties up this signature silhouette.

R9,000

Jordan 3

The Air Jordan 3 “Katrina” may not be an original colorway of the legendary model from 1988, but it’s still one of the most celebrated. It was first produced in very limited quantities to raise money for Hurricane Katrina relief efforts in 2005. After becoming highly sought after in the following years, the Air Jordan 3 “Katrina” finally received a wider release in 2018, much to the delight of Air Jordan collectors everywhere. The unique Jordan 3 colorway features a white leather upper with Fire Red detailing. Cement Grey accents the signature elephant print paneling. The clean look is ready to become a regular member of your Air Jordan collection.

R8,000

Jordan 4

Originally debuting in 1989 and re-releasing multiple times over the years, the Air Jordan 4 Retro OG GS 'Fire Red' (2020) brings back the OG colourway in its truest form since its debut. The shoes upper is constructed of leather, finished primarily in white and accented by Fire Red on the eyelets, tongue tag and inner lining. Black offers contrast throughout the wings, heel tab and midsole. Finally the pair is completed by a fire red, white, and grey sole which includes visible Air in the heel for cushioning..

R7,000

Jordan 5

The women’s air Jordan 5 Gore-Tex ‘Off-Noir’ brings a weather-resistant build to the shoe that Michael Jordan wore during the 1989 – 1990 NBA season. A faded black finish permeates the nubuck upper, equipped with quarter-panel netting and a waterproof Gore-Tex membrane. A crimson Jumpman adorns the heel and silver reflective tongue, while the lateral heel is marked with Gore-Tex branding on the left shoe and a ‘23’ on the right. A black polyurethane midsole features red shark tooth detailing and a visible Air-sole unit in the heel. Underfoot, the translucent rubber outsole is treated to a vintage pre-yellowed finish.

R6,000

Jordan 6

The Air Jordan 6 “DMP 2020” is a Winter 2020 re-release of the classic “Defining Moments Pack” that first appeared in 2006. One of the most celebrated and coveted two-shoe sets ever from Jordan Brand, the Defining Moments Pack celebrated Michael Jordan’s first NBA Championship and contains an Air Jordan 6 and Air Jordan 11, both of which sport a premium redesign. The Air Jordan 6 features a stealthy black nubuck upper with a gold-accented Jumpman on the tongue and gold trim on the midsole that provides a touch of opulent styling. For 2020, Jordan Brand opted to bring back the Jordan 6 from the original pack in all its glory.

R5,000

Jordan 7

The Air Jordan 7 'Bordeaux' is regarded to be the most distinctive of the five initial colorways released in 1992. The sneaker was retro'd only once in 2011 before the 2015 Retro. As part of Nike's 'Remastered' collection, the 2015 retro returns the shoe to its initial glory. The sneaker features a color-blocked black and gray nubuck top that meets a true-to - the-original black combination on black midsole / outsole.

R4,000

Jordan 8

Originally released in 2003 as the first-ever non-original colourway of the Air Jordan 8, the “Chrome” look was back again in 2015 for a second round. The colourway features a predominantly black upper with Light Graphite and chrome accents for a clean and tough look to the iconic silhouette.

R3,000

Jordan 9

This version of the Air Jordan 9 Retro is nicknamed "Space Jam" but at first glance, it's hard to tell why, with the colourway appearing to be the standard red, white and black. It turns out these kicks were the ones that Bugs Bunny and friends had to retrieve from Jordan's house so he could get back in game shape to defeat the Monsters.

R2,000

Jordan 10

Released in 2014 as a new colorway, the Air Jordan 10 'Cool Grey' was originally designed as a spin-off of the classic OG colourway, the Air Jordan 10 'Chicago.' The Air Jordan 10 'Cool Grey' is part of a group of styles released in 2014 that brought back and utilized one of Nike's most popular old-school colours, 'Infrared.' The shoe features a grey premium tumbled leather, Air Jordan 10 striped pattern with infrared accents on the outsole, and the Jumpman logo on the heel.

R1,000
